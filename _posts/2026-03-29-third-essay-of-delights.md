<p>
It wasn’t as easy to find delights today. I find that staying at home means I don’t do very much, or at least I don’t do many delightful things. I want to lie in the grass at night and stare up at the sky while the Earth spins me away from the stars, and I can just pretend that it’s not really me who’s moving. I can pretend that I can keep this moment, and nothing will change, but secretly, I am both excited and terrified for things to change. I am very scared.
</p>
<p>
This morning, as I sat at my white wicker-patterened desk that’s chipping and revealing brown in the form of countless constellations, the sun fractured into a thousand rainbows that painted my desk and arms. There are flowers on that window, translucent ones that cast rainbow prisms on the walls when the sun is high in the sky and wake me with their love for light, bright and early. 
</p>
<p>
I sat in bed for a while before that, the light, fluffy comforter wrapped around me, and I wondered what to do. I watched a video about a Neolithic matriarchy and a short video about the supposed remains of the famous musketeer d’Artagnan, who historians believe was shot in the throat with a musket ball. Interesting and delightful.
</p>
<p>
Having had enough of my room, I ventured out to take my cat, Fork (delight), out for a walk in her little pink suitcase (also a delight), and the scent of dryer sheets wafting up from clothes strung out in the sun was familiar, and how can I not dream of long summer days when the breeze swirls that fragrance around me?
</p>
<p>
Dexter, a little—actually very big—dog, joined us in the yard, and as he inspected his surroundings with inexplicable focus, his nubby little tail cast a shadow on his long torso, a small delight. I came to stand beside him, but he turned towards me and his big brown paws splayed out in front of him as he challenged me to a chase. I obliged, partially because I had been feeling slothful, but also because that tiny nub began to wag with enthusiasm.
</p>
<p>
The pinkish-red veins of tiny green buds sprouting from an otherwise skeletal cluster of branches brought a promise of warmer days, but I still let the cold air sneak up the puffy sleeves of my coat, and surprisingly, it was a delight.
</p>
<p>
There are so many old things living in the backyard. I never really see them, and my eyes usually just wash over the whole scene without actually clinging to the pieces that stay. I’ve only ever looked for things that might leave or change. Hopefully, that changes. Of the old, I noticed the little yellow chair sitting in the sun, a chair that used to match a small table that I would eat at while watching TV. Please cut my food into hearts again. Or I can do it myself, why not? That would be delightful. The basketball against the fence has faded to eraser pink. The basketball hoop isn’t there, and so the evidence of all the games of HORSE that I lost is gone. I’m grateful. I can pretend I have said, “nothing but net,” countless times in my life, or that I never inched forward to combat the disadvantage of my height with a shorter distance.
</p>
<p>
An easy way to say I care is by sharing my space and my time. I take it for granted too often that the people I care for know that I’m thinking of them. So, showing my care is another delight.
</p>
<p>
A tangle of stems and roots and branches of varying browns, deep red stains, dusty browns, and greys that glow both white and cool brown in the half-light, half-shadow of afternoon. Delightful. I want to see every shade, every hue, and every wash of brown. 
</p>
<p>
The erosion of soil and the tree stump that used to be a full circle of wooden rings, now just a crescent of wood jutting out of a mound, no longer a sturdy stage for two front paws as Dexter elevates himself to bark at anything and everything. I hope change can always be delight, or just eventually, indirectly, in the illusion of causality, makes me happy. 
</p>
<p>
With all my looking down, it’s rare that I tilt my head up to look at the sky, but above me was a clear, true blue sky with a few strands of wispy cloud stretching across. 
</p>
<p>
Back in bed, the warmth radiating up from the heated blanket beneath me is a delight. Chasing Fork around the house and watching her big, round green eyes follow my game of peekaboo is always a delight.
</p>
<p>
I started this by saying it was hard to find delights, but maybe what I mean is that this day has had more than delights. Even if it’s hard, there are delights to be found even on slow, scary days. There’s a delight in how easily I find a comfortable position in this chair, even though I know my foot will go numb if I stay like this too long. There’s a delight in writing about all these delights. There’s a heart-fluttering delight in sharing my corner of delights.
</p>
