<p>
Today, I had a rough start. In spite of that, or maybe just along with that, I found many delights as my day went on. 
</p>
<p>
I went on a walk with a lovely friend and Cassie, her even lovelier dog (a statement I don’t think she’d object to), and what better way to spend my midday than to walk with Cassie as she sniffed her way through the woods. The ground was covered in a thick layer of leaves, and the sun was out despite the hand-numbing weather. We crossed a wooden bridge that helped us over a thin stream of water, and it was wonderful in the way that standing on a bridge is. Beneath you is solid wood, but just under that is a cushion of air, and beneath that is a measly flow of water. Purple flowers are beginning to poke their heads out of the frosty grass, more beige than green, an undeniable delight.
</p>
<p>
We went to Panera for lunch, and while filling a cup with a mix of papaya passion tea and unsweetened iced tea, a man working hard on his coffee mixture told us about the coffee his wife had made him that morning. He had added Splenda and cinnamon only to feel a strange spiciness in his throat and realize he had added cayenne (which he pronounced shayenne) instead of cinnamon. He explained that he needed to perfect his Panera coffee mixture to make up for that experience, and then told us to have a great day. It’s incredible how he simply tried again. What an unexpected delight.
</p>
<p>
Reassurance and happiness from a friend or two were much-needed delights that lifted a bit of the ache that’s tried to carve more space for itself within my chest.
</p>
<p>
A sense that maybe things will be okay, even if they don’t seem to be right now, felt more meaningful than the forever blanket statement, ‘everything will be okay,’ especially from a friend who believes me to be completely whole and enough and safe just as I am. I am delighted to share time with someone who believes in me even when I feel only disappointed in myself. I want her to know I’m incredibly proud of her and always astonished by her wisdom, and offer a simple thank you for being a wonderful friend.
</p>
<p>
Walking downtown and back, even though the wind seemed to be against us both times, a conversation in CVS, like middle-aged adults standing in the middle of a grocery store and somehow managing to block half the aisle with their strange orientation. I ordered salted caramel ice cream in a small size, but it came back to me towering, with three scoops in the tiny cone. It was a pleasant mix of coconut and caramel with little flecks of vanilla bean. The dog sticking its shaggy brown head out the window as I finished off the last crunchy bit of cone was certainly a delight.
</p>
<p>
Painting my nails the shade of ‘congrats’, a sparkling blend of blue and pink glitters, and watching them shimmer in the orange evening sun. Even now, looking at my fingers as they flit across the keyboard and finding the brightest yellow-white sun captured in every sparkle is delightful. So delightful that I keep pausing to admire them once again. 
</p>
<p>
Cassie’s beautiful head pushed between my knees as I bent over her to give her head scratches, her snout incredibly close to my face as she gave my eyes a thorough examination, and really, when is she not a delight?
</p>
<p>
The crinkled wrapper of a cinnamon toast muffin is sitting beside me as I write, and in every bite, I find myself believing that I am wonderfully lucky despite it all, or maybe just within it all.
</p>
