<p>
It’s been a while since I’ve written one of these, mostly because being at school is like having the kind of cough you try to stifle and end up choking on until your eyes water. I want to get out, but I know there is so much to miss. Not the school, really, but the people and their strangeness. It’s been kind of hard, but today held many delights.
</p>
<p>
I woke up warm, with my covers all the way up to my chin and a pocket of warmth enveloping me from head to toe. I had a slow start, but only because I took my time. I opened the front door to see someone wonderful waiting to come in, so of course, I let him in. What’s better than a slow day spent in warm arms, looking up at sparkling eyes?
</p>
<p>
We assembled a zen garden, cementing the base together with a glue pen. Glue pens are delightful, and so reminiscent of younger days filled with art classes in the warm, fading golden light of just after lunch. I miss those classes, and I miss one of my art teachers who let me stay in the classroom after the school day had ended and just talk or make little crafts, because what else are hands for? I made tinfoil balls, shiny, round, and cold in my hands, and she loved them. She helped me poke a hole through one of them to make a pendant for a necklace, and it mattered more than anything ever could. I had this ring, with a little circle of amethyst and a missing gem in the center, an absence I didn’t understand until she asked to see it when we were making cupcakes out of clay. Her careful fingers turned it over, and she asked me if she could try to set a small ruby in the middle (jewelry-making being a hobby of hers). 
</p>
<p>
The prongs were broken, so she never managed to add a stone, but I loved everything more just because. 
</p>
<p>
We filled the garden with green sand, pandas, small stones, and a few other ornaments, but the sand really steals the show. It’s delightful, drawing wavy patterns in sand, listening to the wooden tools crunch down, and smoothing it out with the miniature rake. We watched something bright and springy before going out for dinner. A few minutes of walking, then warm food, and the tip of my nose turned itchy from the smoke.
</p>
<p>
I love rubbing my eyes. I just saw a video of a slow loris, and wow, their eyes are huge. They're big and brown, and the rest of them is brown too, but not the same shade of brown. Their eyes are like light caught in the eyes of someone who says their brown eyes are poop brown, even if they catch light and become smooth fragments of amber.
</p>
<p>
I want to hold more things in my hands because that’s what they’re made for in the end.
</p>
